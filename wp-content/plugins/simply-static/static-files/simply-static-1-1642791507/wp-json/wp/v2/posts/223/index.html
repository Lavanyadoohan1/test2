Rishabh Pant, an Indian wicketkeeper\/batsman, smashed 85 in the second One-Day International (ODI) against South Africa on Friday (January 21).<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Pant took just 71 balls to make 85 and appeared to be on his way to his first ODI century before a loose shot against Tabraiz Shamshi cost him his wicket.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

However, his 85-run home run was enough to put him in the record books.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

His 85th ODI score in South Africa is now the highest by an Indian wicketkeeper.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Pant passed Rahul Dravid to become the 78th player to reach the milestone. MS Dhoni (65), Rahul Dravid (62), and Saba Karim are the other Indians on the list (55).<\/p>\n\n\n\n

India needs to win the second ODI to keep the series alive after losing the first. India won the toss and chose to bat first, but Dhawan fell early and Kohli also fell for naught.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Pant and Rahul came together and put on a 115-run stand for the third wicket, but SA struck again, taking back-to-back wickets of Rahul and Pant to end India’s innings.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
