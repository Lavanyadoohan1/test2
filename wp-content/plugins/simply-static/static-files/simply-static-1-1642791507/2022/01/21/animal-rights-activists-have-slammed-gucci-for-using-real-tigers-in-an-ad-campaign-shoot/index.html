Animal rights activists have slammed Gucci for using real tigers in an ad campaign shoot.

ByAdmin

Jan 21, 2022

Gucci, an Italian luxury fashion house, has come under fire on social media for using real tigers in their advertising campaign. The Gucci Tiger Collection debuted earlier this month to commemorate the Chinese Year of the Tiger, which begins on February 1st. Many animal rights activists, including the founder of Big Cat Rescue, Carole Baskin, and PETA, were outraged by their decision to use real tigers for the shoot.

View this post on Instagram

A post shared by Gucci Official (@gucci)

One of the images from Gucci’s recent campaign features two tigers, one perched on a piano and the other posing on a rug. In another Instagram video shared by Gucci, a tiger is seen walking.

View this post on Instagram

A post shared by Gucci Official (@gucci)

Carole Baskin, an animal activist, called it the “worst possible message.” “This type of ad campaign sends the worst possible message: that tigers are disposable commodities to be used for capitalism and discarded at will,” she told Yahoo News. She went on to say that it diminishes the tiger’s majesty and makes Gucci look bad.

World Animal Protection US also expressed their dismay on Twitter. “Gucci is sending the wrong message by promoting tigers as pets and luxury items when they are wild animals that belong in the wild,” according to the tweet.

The People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals (PETA) also slammed Gucci on Twitter earlier this month.

When will Gucci learn that animals are not props!

Tweet at @gucci and tell them to STOP using wild animals in their ads. pic.twitter.com/QnH7xjtZzS

— PETA (@peta) January 6, 2022

Several netizens also expressed their opinions in the comments section of Gucci’s posts. “Please leave the wildlife alone,” one user wrote. “Tigers are beautiful in nature, not in marketing campaigns,” another commenter writes.

Gucci, on the other hand, stated in their disclaimer that no animals were harmed during the shoot. “Tigers were photographed and filmed in a separate safe environment in accordance with Gucci’s policies before being featured in the campaign,” the disclaimer continued. Netizens, on the other hand, are clearly not convinced.
